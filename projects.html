Research

Kawasak.AI

At 14, I joined the UCSD Kawasaki Disease Research Center/Rady Children's Hospital and developed the first image recognition AI for early diagnosis.

Notable experiences: Grand Award 3rd place at the International Science & Engineering Fair (2x ISEF finalist), Sigma Xi Top Presentor (inducted into Sigma Xi), Scripps "Young Scientist" Award (presented at graduate school symposium). I was also invited to present at the triennial International KD Symposium in Tokyo.

Cybersecurity

CyberPatriot XIII National Champion

After becoming a 3x National Finalist, my team won 1st place out of 2,633 teams in the Open Division National Finals for CyberPatriot, the world's largest cyber defense competition. I also taught defensive cybersecurity in Windows OS and computer networking, and created weekly virtual images (VMWare) for 120+ students.

Certified in CompTIA IT Fundamentals+, GIAC Foundational Cybersecurity Technologies (SEC275).

Nonprofit

Zipline Theory 501(c)(3)

Founded non-profit teaching students across 30+ countries to build solutions to the world's pressing problems in new fields.

I sourced and obtained funding from the Department of Defense, National Center for Women in Technology, Apple, Google, Northrop Grumman, Amazon, Walmart, Televisa Foundation, and Cognizant USA Foundation to lead a program teaching cybersecurity to girls of military families.

Fencing

Competitive saber fencer

I was selected as a Top 20 fencer to represent USA at the 2021 Romania World Cup (U17 European Circuit), and placed 4th place in team event and 5th place individually (out of 200 fencers from 25 countries).

Notable experiences: Captain of Junior Olympic Silver medal team (2020), Top 32 in Division I at December North American Cup (2021), Junior Champion at UCSD Bladerunner (2021).

Podcast

I host podcast with 250+ downloads/episode and write a newsletter breaking down technology, startups, and innovation. Previous guests include the creator of the 1st COVID-19 website, Harvard Business School associate professors, and founder of the largest community of student hackers/builders.

Featured at RSA Conference, channel coverage of the largest cybersecurity conferences including DEFCON and BlackHat.

Writing

Creative Writer

In 4th grade, I was encouraged to submit a short story to a state writing competition, which won 1st place in California and was recognized by Assemblymember Brian Maienschein. Since then, I have written a personal narrative piece published in the New York Times (selected out of 11,000 essays by NYT journalists), and became a 2x regional Gold Key winner in the Scholastic Art & Writing Competition. I serve as President and Editor-in-Chief of The Featheralist, an award-winning Columbia Press Association Gold literary publication.
